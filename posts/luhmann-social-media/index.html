Mon, Jan 1, 0001

If we are to take a luhmannian approach and look at the phenomenon using a systems theory lens, we must be more cautious when postulating a causal relationship. Then we might understand the growing political radicalisation and polarisation not as an effect in the political system or the economic system deternined by the chamges in the methods of communication, but rather as changes in all systems influencing each other through perturbations in a complex way, resulting in communication bottlenecks. Such bottlenecks have been merely mitigated by the emergence of new media. If we are to engender this vision, a very different perspective emerges. In other words, people do not get manipulated by new media into rioting or believing falsehoods. Rather, they use new media to communicate within regions of systems that conform to their own already existing biases amd reinforce them. They enable communication by restricting access to certain information that would result in bottlenecks and enable the flow of other kinds of more desirable information.
